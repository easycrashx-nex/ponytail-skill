# Define

## Load When

Load for vague, incomplete, conflicting, underspecified, or idea-stage requests. Skip for mechanical or already unambiguous work.

## Minimal Flow

1. State the likely intent and the important missing information.
2. Use **ExtendThings**: complete only gaps required for the idea to work—user, outcome, success, constraints, integration, failure handling, verification, and explicit non-goals.
3. Mark assumptions. Ask one question only when a gap would materially change direction, cost, or risk. Do not silently choose a regulated jurisdiction, irreversible constraint, or target user when that choice changes required behavior, cost, or risk.
4. When several credible directions exist, use **ALT3**:
   - Minimal
   - Balanced
   - Ambitious
5. Compare value, effort, risk, and reversibility; recommend one.
6. Produce a compact intent/spec: objective, user, success criteria, constraints, assumptions, scope, non-goals, and verification.

Do not invent three options when fewer are credible. Do not replace the user's idea with your own.

## Higher-Risk Depth

For costly or consequential ideas, test assumptions against existing repository evidence and identify the cheapest experiment that could disprove the idea.

## Stop

Stop when the user intent is coherent enough to plan and remaining uncertainty does not change the next step.

## Verify

- Required gaps are covered.
- Assumptions and non-goals are explicit.
- The recommendation is smaller than or equal to the original scope unless evidence justifies expansion.
